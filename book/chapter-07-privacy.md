# Chapter 7 – Privacy

**Your data, your life – privacy is security**

---

> *"Arguing that you don't care about the right to privacy because you have nothing to hide is no different from saying you don't care about free speech because you have nothing to say."*  
> – Edward Snowden

---

We live in an age in which data is the most valuable commodity in the world. Every time you google something, scroll through Instagram, or open an app, data about you is collected, sold, and analyzed. That feels abstract – until it gets concrete.

For Bitcoin users, privacy is not a philosophical question. It's a security question. Anyone who knows you own Bitcoin knows there is something worth taking. If they also know your name and address, they have everything they need. The combination of Bitcoin ownership and lack of privacy is a real risk – there have already been targeted robberies, extortions, and kidnappings worldwide in which criminals identified their victims through leaked data.

Privacy doesn't only protect you from criminals. It protects you from data misuse, identity theft, targeted advertising, and state surveillance. And it's easier to achieve than most people think.

---

## What Bitcoin Has to Do With Your Privacy

Bitcoin transactions are pseudonymous, not anonymous. Every transaction is publicly visible on the blockchain – to anyone, worldwide, forever. An address has no name. But once someone knows which address belongs to you, they can see your entire transaction history – retroactively and going forward.

That's why: privacy in Bitcoin must be actively created. It is not the default. The most important first step – KYC-free purchasing – is already covered in Chapter 3. Here we'll deal with everything that comes after: your device, your browser, your communication.

---

## Your Device Is the Foundation

Before thinking about browsers or messengers, it's worth looking at the operating system. Windows and macOS regularly send telemetry data to Microsoft and Apple. Those who are serious use Linux for financially sensitive activities – or **Tails OS**, an operating system that boots from a USB stick, leaves no traces, and forgets everything after shutdown.

It's similar with smartphones: Android from Google and iOS from Apple are both no privacy champions. Alternatives like **GrapheneOS** (grapheneos.org) or **LineageOS** run without Google services and give you back control.

## Privacy Tips for Daily Life

| Area | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Browser | Brave (brave.com) or Firefox with uBlock Origin. For maximum anonymity: Tor Browser |
| VPN | Mullvad (mullvad.net) – accepts Bitcoin, no log files. Alternative: LN VPN |
| Search engine | Brave Search (search.brave.com) or DuckDuckGo – no tracking, no profile |
| Messenger | Signal (signal.org) – open source, post-quantum. Alternative: SimpleX Chat |
| Email | Proton Mail (proton.me) or Tutanota (tuta.com) – encrypted, open source |
| 2FA | Aegis (Android) or Raivo (iOS) – never via SMS |
| Passwords | Bitwarden, KeePass |

---

## Where Do I Start?

If you only want to take one single step: install **Brave** as your browser and **Signal** as your messenger. That alone will improve your data situation significantly – and it costs you five minutes.

If you want to go deeper: change your search engine, set up a password manager, and have a look at Tails OS.
