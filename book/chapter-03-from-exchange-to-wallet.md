# Chapter 3 – From Exchange to Your Own Wallet

**The switch in 5 steps – no prior knowledge required**

---

This is the most important step you can take as a Bitcoin user. And it's easier than it sounds. Let's walk through it together.

> **Start With a Test Amount**  
> Never send everything at once. Begin with a small amount – for example the equivalent of 10 to 20 euros. Check that it arrives. Only then send the rest. A mistake in the address is irreversible.

---

**Step 1 – Set up the wallet**  
Download a recommended wallet: Aqua Wallet for smartphones, Sparrow for desktop. Set up the wallet – on first launch a seed is generated automatically.

**Step 2 – Back up the seed immediately**  
Write down all 12 or 24 words by hand and in the correct order on paper. No screenshot. Store the paper safely.

**Step 3 – Copy the receiving address**  
In your wallet, tap "Receive." You'll see a Bitcoin address – a long string of characters, usually also shown as a QR code. Copy this address.

**Step 4 – Send a test amount from the exchange**  
Go to your exchange. Select "Withdraw" or "Send," paste your wallet address, and send the test amount. Check the address twice: compare the first and last 6 characters. Wait for confirmation (10–60 minutes).

**Step 5 – Verify and send the rest**  
Open your wallet and check whether the amount has arrived. If yes: everything works correctly. Now you can gradually transfer the rest of your Bitcoin from the exchange.

> 💡 **Well done!**  
> Once the test amount has arrived in your wallet, you officially have your first own Bitcoin address and are step by step becoming your own bank. This is the most important step.

## Buying Bitcoin – the First Satoshis

> **What Are Satoshis?**  
> 1 Bitcoin = 100,000,000 Satoshis (sats for short). You don't have to buy a whole Bitcoin. 10 euros is enough to get started and try everything out.

When buying Bitcoin, there is a decision you should make consciously from the start: with or without identity verification. We recommend: without. Not because it's more convenient – but because it's safer.

> ⚠️ **KYC Can Be Dangerous**  
> KYC means a platform stores your ID and links it to your Bitcoin purchases. If that platform gets hacked – and it happens regularly – criminals know your name, your address, and how much Bitcoin you own. There have already been cases worldwide of kidnapping, extortion, and assaults in which victims were identified through leaked exchange data. Your data is your risk.

### Recommended KYC-Free Options

The following options allow you to buy without uploading an ID. They are not more difficult – just a little less well known.

- **GetBittr** (getbittr.com) – SEPA transfer, no ID, direct to your own wallet
- **Peach Bitcoin** (peachbitcoin.com) – P2P app, no KYC, very easy to use
- **Hodl Hodl** (hodlhodl.com) – P2P platform in the browser, no KYC
- **Bisq 2** (bisq.network) – decentralized, open source, desktop, maximum privacy
- **Local Bitcoin meetups** – in-person exchange for cash, no data trail
- **kycnot.me** – constantly updated overview of all KYC-free providers

> **KYC – Know Your Customer**  
> Some platforms require an ID upload above certain amounts – this is legally mandated. Check the limits beforehand. For regular purchases, it's worth using a platform without KYC.

## DCA – the Smart Savings Plan

Instead of buying a large amount in one go, many experienced Bitcoiners buy small amounts regularly: weekly or monthly, regardless of price. This strategy is called Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA).

| DCA advantage | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| No market timing needed | You don't have to find the "right" entry moment. |
| Psychologically calmer | Price drops are automatically used to your advantage. |
| Disciplined saving | Like an automatic savings plan, just without a bank. |
| Tax-clear (DE) | After 1 year of holding, gains are tax-free in Germany. |

> ⚠️ **Important Tax Note**  
> In Germany: Bitcoin gains after a holding period of at least 12 months are tax-free. Short-term sales are taxable as private disposal transactions. Please consult your own tax advisor. Rules differ from country to country.
