# Chapter 4 – The Lightning Network

**Bitcoin in everyday life – fast, cheap, worldwide**

---

Bitcoin on the main blockchain is like an international bank transfer: secure, immutable – but not ideal for spontaneous everyday payments. The Lightning Network solves that.

## What Is Lightning?

Lightning is a second-layer solution: a network of payment channels built on top of Bitcoin. Transactions happen directly between users – within seconds, with minimal fees (often less than a cent), without waiting for the main blockchain every time.

> *"Lightning is like a bar tab between friends. You go back and forth all evening – and only settle up at the end. The final result then lands on the blockchain."*

Picture this: you pay for your coffee, your lunch, and an evening beer – all with Bitcoin, all in seconds, all at minimal cost. That's Lightning today, in many cities around the world.

---

## Two Wallet Types in Everyday Use

Important to understand: your long-term savings wallet and your Lightning wallet are two different things – like a savings account and the loose change in your pocket.

| Bitcoin wallet (savings) | Lightning wallet (everyday spending) |
|---|---|
| For larger amounts | For everyday amounts |
| Transactions take minutes | Transactions take seconds |
| Higher fees per transaction | Minimal fees (often under 1 cent) |
| Aqua Wallet, Sparrow, hardware | Aqua Wallet (hybrid), Phoenix, Zeus, Breez, Wallet of Satoshi |

---

## Recommended Lightning Wallets

### Non-Custodial (recommended)

You hold your own keys – even on Lightning.

- **Aqua Wallet** – also supports Lightning (slightly higher fees compared to custodial solutions)
- **Phoenix Wallet** (phoenix.acinq.co) – user-friendly, automatic channel management, ideal for beginners
- **Breez** (breez.technology) – easy to use, with built-in point-of-sale for merchants
- **Zeus** (zeusln.app) – advanced, can be connected to your own node

### Custodial (easy entry)

A third party holds the Lightning keys – acceptable for very small everyday amounts.

- **Blink Wallet** (blink.sv) – very simple, based in El Salvador, holds a banking license
- **Wallet of Satoshi** (walletofsatoshi.com) – very simple, based in Australia, no banking license

> ⚠️ **Custodial Lightning – Pocket Money Only**  
> Custodial Lightning wallets are like cash in your pocket: handy for small amounts, but not for larger sums. Anything above a few euros belongs in a non-custodial wallet.

## How to Top Up a Lightning Wallet

You top up your Lightning wallet by sending a small amount of Bitcoin from your savings wallet:

1. Open your Lightning wallet and tap "Receive"
2. Depending on the selected symbol, the app will show you either a Bitcoin address or a Lightning address. Lightning addresses are usually marked with a lightning bolt, while Bitcoin addresses carry a Bitcoin symbol or are labeled "Onchain."
3. Go to your Bitcoin wallet or exchange and send a small amount to the address shown.
4. After confirmation, your Lightning balance is available.

> **Telling Addresses Apart**  
> You can also distinguish Lightning and Bitcoin addresses by their format: Lightning starts with "ln…," while Bitcoin addresses begin differently depending on type (e.g. "bc1…," "1…," or "3…").

> 💡 **Your First Lightning Payment**  
> Most Lightning wallets show you a QR code when receiving. Someone else with a Lightning wallet can simply scan that code and send you satoshis in seconds. Try it out at a Bitcoin meetup!
