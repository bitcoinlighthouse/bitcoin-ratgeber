# Chapter 9 – 21 Lessons

**What really matters on your way into the Bitcoin rabbit hole**

---

The best is saved for last. Here are 21 thoughts from my Bitcoin experience – gathered over many years, many mistakes, and many conversations with beginners and old hands.

---

**1 – Study Bitcoin first**  
Put your first money into books about Bitcoin rather than directly into Bitcoin. Understand what you're buying – and why.

**2 – Live up to personal responsibility**  
Not your keys, not your coins sounds trivial – until you realize why it matters. Custody your own keys.

**3 – Don't trust, verify**  
Stay skeptical of everyone: exchanges, neo-banks, banks, influencers. Check for yourself before you act.

**4 – Explore every aspect**  
Bitcoin touches monetary policy, history, psychology, economics, and philosophy. The more you understand, the more confident your decisions feel.

**5 – Ignore the FUD**  
The media regularly declares Bitcoin dead. 99 percent of Bitcoin news is noise. What's interesting is the decade, not the day.

**6 – Understand incentives**  
Everyone telling you something about Bitcoin has an agenda. Always ask: what does this person stand to gain?

**7 – Austrian School**  
Engage with the Austrian School of economics. It explains much of what the mainstream leaves out.

**8 – Stay grounded**  
When Bitcoin goes up: stay humble. Don't be the person who always says "I told you so."

**9 – Think in decades**  
Don't judge Bitcoin by the day, the month, or the year. Think in decades. Short-term volatility is not a signal.

**10 – Have meaningful work**  
Even if Bitcoin reaches ten million – have a task that's useful to others. Money does not replace meaning.

**11 – Don't forget what's essential**  
Bitcoin is fascinating. But friends, family, health, and love come first.

**12 – DCA instead of gambling**  
Stop speculating. Learn what dollar cost averaging means – and do it regularly.

**13 – Uninstall price apps**  
Watching the price daily leads to bad decisions. Bitcoin is volatile and cyclical. Let it go.

**14 – Understand the media**  
Learn how the media works. Why do they write what they write? The answer often lies in the business model.

**15 – Think independently**  
Bitcoin was created by critical thinkers. Think critically too – even toward the Bitcoin bubble itself.

**16 – Convince no one**  
Share your enthusiasm. But accept that not everyone wants to swallow the orange pill. That's okay.

**17 – Hodl**  
Hold for as long as you can. Engage with the concept of time preference – it changes how you think about money.

**18 – No shitcoins**  
Don't buy other cryptocurrencies. Don't trade. Every other cryptocurrency is fiat money with another name.

**19 – Act by principle**  
Act consistently and according to your values. Be like Bitcoin: reliable, transparent, immutable.

**20 – Stay kind**  
Be kind to everyone – even to those who reject Bitcoin or don't understand it. Your behavior represents the movement.

**21 – Think about scarcity**  
There will never be more than 21 million Bitcoin. Really try to understand what that means – for time, for value, for the future.

---

**STAY HUMBLE AND STACK SATS!**

*Every journey begins with a first step – and you have just taken it.*
