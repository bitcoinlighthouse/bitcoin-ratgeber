# Chapter 5 – Security

**Protect yourself from the most common attacks**

---

As your own bank, you carry full responsibility for security. That sounds intimidating – but it isn't. Most attacks are well known and easy to avoid if you know a few simple rules.

---

## Hot vs. Cold Storage

| Hot Storage | Cold Storage |
|---|---|
| Connected to the internet | Offline – no internet access |
| Quick access | Maximum security |
| For everyday amounts and Lightning | For long-term custody |
| Software wallet on smartphone or PC | Hardware wallet, paper wallet, steel wallet |
| Risk: hack, malware | Risk: physical loss, fire, theft |

Rule of thumb: what you'd spend in a normal month can sit in a hot wallet. Anything beyond that belongs in cold storage.

---

## Backup Methods for the Seed

### Paper Wallet (Paper Backup)

The simplest method: seed words written by hand on paper. Use a document-grade pen. Main risks: fire and water. Ideal as a first level of backup.

### Steel Wallet (Metal Backup)

Seed words stamped or engraved into metal. Survives even house fires and floods. Recommended for anyone holding more than a few hundred euros in Bitcoin.

- **Cryptosteel Capsule** – proven classic
- **Seedor Safe Starter Set** – affordable and simple
- **Bitplates** – German product
- **DIY: steel washers with letter punches** – cheapest option

> 💡 **Separate Locations**  
> Never store your seed and your hardware wallet in the same place. Whoever finds both has everything. Seed at home, hardware wallet elsewhere – or the other way around.

## The Most Common Attacks – and Your Defense

### 1. Phishing

Fake websites or emails that look like real services. Goal: your password or – worse – your seed.

- Bookmark important pages, never click on links from emails
- Check URLs carefully – typo domains are common (e.g. `bltbox.swiss` instead of `bitbox.swiss`)
- No legitimate support ever asks for your seed – never, under no circumstances

### 2. Clipboard Hijacking

Malware on your computer replaces a copied Bitcoin address with an address belonging to the attacker. You think you're sending to yourself – and end up sending to the scammer.

> 💡 **Defense: Always Check the Address**  
> After pasting, always compare the first 6 and the last 6 characters with the original. Hardware wallets display the address on their own screen – that's a key security advantage.

### 3. Too-Good-to-Be-True Offers

- "Send 1 Bitcoin, get 2 back" – always a scam, no exception
- Celebrities giving away Bitcoin – always a scam
- Someone offers to recover lost Bitcoin – always a scam
- Strangers offering help with Bitcoin problems – always a scam

### 4. SIM Swapping

Attackers take over your phone number and bypass SMS-based two-factor authentication.

- Don't use SMS-based 2FA for Bitcoin accounts
- Use an authenticator app: **Aegis** (Android, open source) or **Raivo** (iOS)

### Additional Protective Measures

- Password manager: **Bitwarden** (open source) or **KeePassDX**
- Different passwords for every service
- Update wallet apps regularly

> **FUD – Fear, Uncertainty and Doubt**  
> Media outlets regularly declare Bitcoin dead or banned. On 99bitcoins.com/bitcoin-obituaries you can see how often this has already been claimed – and how often it has been wrong.
